Why Lottery Shrinkage Is the Hardest Kind to Catch

Lottery tickets are basically cash. Once a pack is activated, every ticket inside has a real dollar value - and that makes theft prevention retail uniquely difficult for lottery operators. The most common forms are quiet: missing books from the backstock, micro-scratching to test for winners, voided sales that never get returned to the display, win/lose swaps where employees pocket payouts. Without proper inventory shrinkage prevention in place, these losses pile up shift by shift until the monthly reconciliation reveals the gap. By then, the trail is cold and the money is gone.
